Don't listen to the banks. Build with them.

Embedded Finance Build Day. One day, real APIs, real mentors, real fintech agents shipped by evening. Stockholm's fintech ecosystem, hands on for once.

You leave most fintech events with opinions. You leave this one with something built.

Stockholm has no shortage of fintech events. Panels, keynotes, coffee, business cards. Build Day inverts that. The people who usually speak from stages sit at your table instead — engineers and product leads bring their APIs, their sandboxes and their time. You bring a laptop and a working brain.

The theme

Building fintech agents

AI agents that move money, assess credit, reconcile payments, talk to banking infrastructure. It's the most interesting frontier in embedded finance right now — and almost nobody has hands-on experience with it yet. This is where you get yours.

You don't need to be an AI expert. You need curiosity and one day of focus. Teams form on the day; developers, product people and domain experts all have a role.

Technical partners

Not sponsorship-as-logo-placement. Partners in the room.

Their teams mentor, their infrastructure powers the builds, and they're watching closely for the ideas and the people that stand out. If you've wanted a real conversation with these organizations — the kind that doesn't happen in a conference hallway — this is the format where it happens naturally. You'll be solving a problem together by 10 am.

Froda

SME lending infrastructure — credit and financing embedded where businesses already are.

Worldline

Global payment services — the rails a large share of European transactions run on.

Danske Bank

Nordic banking at scale — the institutional perspective on what agents can and can't touch.

Open Payments

Open banking APIs — account data and payment initiation, ready to build on.

What you take with you

A working build

Relationships that started with work

A real read on embedded finance

  • Product and business people who want to understand embedded finance from the inside
  • Anyone serious about getting into — or ahead in — the Stockholm fintech space
